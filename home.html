About University of Idaho

The University of Idaho is a historic public university, founded in 1889, known for general education, the campus covers an area of 800 acres (about 4856 acres). The university began to enroll graduate programs in 1902, and now has nine graduate schools, 24 doctoral degrees, and 154 undergraduate programs. It has 10 colleges, 5 branch schools and 5 famous research institutes. It was listed by Kiplinger as one of the 100 Most Valuable Public Universities in 2008, and is consistently recognized by U.S. News & World Report as "the nation's top PhD-granting university." The University of Idaho is the No. 1 university in Idaho and No. 3 overall in the northwestern United States. It ranks in the top 11% of comprehensive universities in the United States. It is ranked among the top 25 universities in the United States with the best educational investment value by Fortune Magazine.
